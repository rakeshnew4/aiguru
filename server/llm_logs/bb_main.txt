Let's solve **part (i): 140**.

To find the prime factors, we use a **factor tree** approach. We keep dividing by the smallest prime number possible until we reach 1.

**Step-by-step calculation:**
1. Start with 140. It's even, so divide by 2: $$140 \div 2 = 70$$
2. 70 is also even, so divide by 2 again: $$70 \div 2 = 35$$
3. 35 isn't divisible by 2 or 3. Let's try 5: $$35 \div 5 = 7$$
4. 7 is a prime number, so we divide by 7: $$7 \div 7 = 1$$

Now, collect all the numbers you divided by: 2, 2, 5, and 7.

So, the prime factorization of 140 is:
$$140 = 2 \times 2 \times 5 \times 7$$

We can write this more neatly using exponents:
$$\boxed{140 = 2^2 \times 5 \times 7}$$

**Real-world tip:** This is exactly how computers encrypt secret messages! They use huge numbers and their prime factors to create codes that are super hard to break.
